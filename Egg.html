2.Egg Biryani

Instructions:

  • Marinate the Egg

    • In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice. Allow it to marinate for at least 1-2 hours, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.

  • Cook the Rice:

    • - Rinse the soaked basmati rice under cold water.

    - In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt, bay leaves, cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick.

    - Add the rinsed rice and cook until it's 70-80% cooked. Drain the water and set aside.

  • Prepare Biryani Masala:

    • - In a large pan, heat ghee or oil over medium heat.

    - Add cumin seeds and fennel seeds. Once they splutter, add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.

    - Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

    - Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and biryani masala. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil separates.

    - Add marinated chicken and cook until it's partially cooked.

  • Layering and Dum Cooking:

    • - In a heavy-bottomed pan or biryani pot, layer half of the partially cooked rice.

    - Sprinkle chopped mint leaves and cilantro over the rice layer.

    - Add the remaining chicken masala over the rice layer.

    - Top it with the rest of the partially cooked rice.

    - Garnish with fried onions and a few mint leaves.

    - Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with aluminum foil. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the chicken to cook completely.

  • Serve:

    • - Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork.

    - Serve hot with raita (yogurt side dish) or a simple cucumber and mint salad.
